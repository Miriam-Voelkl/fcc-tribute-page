Oscar Wilde's life

  • 1854 - Oscar Wilde is born in Dublin, Ireland
  • 1874-1878 - Wilde attends Trinity College, Dublin, and then Magdalen College, Oxford, where he distinguishes himself as a classical scholar and becomes known for his wit and flamboyant personality
  • 1881 - Wilde publishes his first collection of poetry, "Poems"
  • 1884 - Marriage to Constance Lloyd, with whom he has two sons, Cyril and Vyvyan
  • 1888 - Publication of Wilde's only novel, "The Picture of Dorian Gray" in Lippincott's Monthly Magazine
  • 1891 - Wilde's play "Salom√©" is published in French
  • 1895 - Wilde's relationship with Lord Alfred Douglas leads to his arrest and subsequent trials for "gross indecency" due to his homosexuality
  • 1897 - Wilde is released from prison and goes into exile in France under the name Sebastian Melmoth
  • 1900 - Oscar Wilde dies of meningitis in Paris aged 46
